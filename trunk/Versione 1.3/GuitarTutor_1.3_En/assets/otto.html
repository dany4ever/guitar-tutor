Chords Structure and Use


A Chord is the composition of three or more notes played simultaneously. The basic composition of an agreement is that of three notes and is called " Triad ". We can divide the agreements in four major categories:

  • Major chords
  • Minor chords
  • Seventh chords
  • Semi-diminished chords

Major chords

Major chords are characterized by the following structure:
  • 1st degree (Major)
  • 3rd degree (Major)
  • 5th degree (Flat)

Minor chords

The minor chords are characterized by the following structure:
  • 1st Degree (Major)
  • 3rd Degree (Minor)
  • 5 degrees (Flat)


These categories are extracted from the main course of triads (major and minor). In this triad, we can add a further degree (the 7th grade on a scale), achieving so an agreement of 7 °.
Chords of 7 degrees are used in blues and jazz and are the backbone of these genres.

Seventh chords

Seventh chords are divided further into three categories:
  • Seventh major
  • Minor seventh
  • Dominant seventh

Major seventh chords

  • 1st Degree (Major)
  • 3rd Degree (Major)
  • 5th Degrees (Flat)
  • 7th Degree (Major)

Minor seventh chords

  • 1st Degree (Major)
  • 3rd Degree (Minor)
  • 5th Degrees(Right)
  • 7th Degree (Minor)

Dominant seventh chords

  • 1st Degree (Major)
  • 3rd Degree (Major)
  • 5th Degree(Flat)
  • 7th Degree(Minor)

Semi-diminished chords

  • 1st Degree (Major)
  • 3rd Degree (Minor)
  • 5th Degree (Diminished)
  • 7th Degree (Minor)


We can also extend this rule to successive degrees to 7 degrees, resulting in chords of 9 °, 10 °, 11 ° 13 ° and 16 °.
This rule is necessary for the construction of so-called "tetrads".
